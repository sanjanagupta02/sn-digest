1 · Fetching papers from arXiv
arXiv.org has a public API at export.arxiv.org/api/query that returns results as XML. We query three categories: astro-ph.HE (high-energy astrophysics), astro-ph.SR (stellar astrophysics), and astro-ph.GA (galaxies). We ask for the 80 most recently submitted papers matching supernova keywords in the title or abstract.
2 · Keyword filtering
After fetching, we keep only papers whose title or abstract contains at least one of these keywords. We also detect individual supernova names like SN 2023ixf or AT2024abc. The matched: label on each card shows you which keyword triggered it.
3 · Tracking what you've already seen
Each paper's arXiv ID is saved in your browser's local storage after you fetch. The next day, papers you've already seen are dimmed — new ones are highlighted. arXiv batches submissions: papers submitted Friday–Sunday all appear on Monday, so "new" means submitted today or yesterday (UTC).
4 · AI analysis
When you click "Generate Analysis," the title and abstract are sent to your chosen AI (Claude or Gemini). The response streams word-by-word as it arrives. Only the title and abstract are sent — no personal data. Your API key stays in your browser.
5 · Export
The Export button copies a Markdown-formatted summary (title, authors, arXiv link, abstract, and any analysis) to your clipboard. Paste it into Obsidian, Notion, or save as a .md file. If clipboard access is blocked it downloads the file directly.
